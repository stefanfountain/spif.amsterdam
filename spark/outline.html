Phase 02

Meditation & reality

### What is reality?

  • What you just did — noticing that the word isn't the thing — is the beginning of meditation.

The art of meditation is a way of getting into touch with reality, and the reason for this is that most civilized people are out of touch with reality because they confuse the world as it is. With the world as they think about it and talk about it and describe it,

  • for, on the one hand, there is the real world and on the other, a whole system of symbols about that world, which we have in our minds.

These are very, very useful symbols. All civilization depends on them. But like all good things, they have their disadvantages. And the principle disadvantage of symbols is that we confuse them with reality.

Take a map. A map is one of the most useful symbols we have — you can navigate by it, plan with it, point to it. But the map is not the country. The map is a picture of the country, drawn from one particular point of view.

And these days, we have several maps of the same place at once. You can open Google Maps and flip between the normal view and the satellite view — two completely different images of the same street. Both useful. Both true. Neither one is the street.

We choose a way to view reality. We do it constantly, without noticing.

Just as we confuse money with actual wealth, and our names about ourselves, our ideas of ourselves, our images of ourselves, with ourselves.

even our words are symbols, they are not reality itself.

all words are made up if you think about it, they are just sounds that we agree to use to represent things.

Reality however - reality is not a concept.

Reality itself is not a symbol.

Reality is <gong sound> .... and we won't give it a name.

Now it's amazing what doesn't exist in the real world, reaility is such a marvelous system of wiggles. In which we describe things and events in the same way as we would project images on a Rorschach plot, or pick out particular groups of stars in the sky and call them constellations as if they were separate groups of stars. Well, there are groups of stars in the mind's eye, in our system of concepts. They are not out there as constellations already grouped in the sky.

Phase 03

Models, not reality, Narcissus and the self-model

### Why we need models

  • Tree isn't a tree.
  • Free isn't freedom

Yet we need models:

  • Imagine figuring out what a chair is every time we see one
  • Imagine identifying each blade of grass
  • Without them we'd get stuck and be paralysed
  • Models compress particularity into something we can act on
  • Models are necessary, but some are wrong.
  • Many are incomplete
  • Remember the words at the start — rain, Portugal, fire, mom, dad, school.
  • Each one lit up a model. Each model was different from the actual thing
  • You see of all the models you carry, the most important is the model of yourself

### The self-model as reflection

You probably know the story of Narcissus...

  • He saw his reflection in the water and fell in love with it
  • At first he didn't know it was him — he reaches in, the figure reaches back, he speaks to it, but can't touch or hold it
  • Eventually he realises: "iste ego sum" — "this is me"
  • Which then deepens the tragedy because he thinks HE IS THE REFLECTION
  • because the image is still flat, still unreachable, still not the living thing.
  • He stays there until he wastes away
  • The usual reading is "he loved himself too much." That's wrong
  • You see ... the myth isn't about self-love at all; it's about the failure to recognize the soul in its own reflection.
  • He longed for someone he could never become
  • He loved a picture of himself until he forgot there was anyone behind it
  • We are all a little Narcissus:
  • We have an image of ourselves — polished, ideal, the version that holds together
  • We stare at it. We forget it's a reflection
  • And the living, breathing, contradictory you — the one actually doing the staring — gets quieter and quieter
  • That's the trap. Not loving yourself too much. Loving the picture of yourself until you forget there's anyone behind it.
  • While we stare at the surface, we miss what's underneath.
  • Youre the one with the brush.

And underneath the polished version, there's something the model can't show you:

  • When you remember a moment, you don't play it back. You rebuild it. Every time. From inside you
  • When you imagine a moment that hasn't happened — your mind builds it the same way. From the same place
  • Memory and imagination come from the same part of you
  • When you picture yourself braver, or kinder, or more alive — you couldn't picture it vividly if the capacity weren't already in you
  • The image is a self-portrait of potential. Drawn by the part of you that already knows the shape
  • That's the one we're going to investigate

Phase 04

Breathing & falling into meditation

### Get into meditation

  • Well, now the easiest way to get into the meditative state is to begin by listening.
  • I want you to listen to the sound of my voice, just as if it were noise.
  • Don't try to make any sense out of what I'm saying because your brain will take care of that automatically.
  • You don't have to try to understand anything, just listen to the sound.

As you pursue that experiment, you will very naturally find that you can't help naming sounds, identifying them — that you will go on thinking.

  • That is to say, talking to yourself inside your head automatically. But it's important you don't try to repress those thoughts by forcing them out of your mind — that will have precisely the same effect as trying to smooth rough water with your hand.

You're just going to disturb it all. The more what you do is this, as you hear sounds coming up in your head.

  • Thoughts, you simply listen to them as part of the general noise going on. Just as you would be listening to the sound of my voice or just as you would be listening to cars going by or to birds chattering outside the window.
  • So look at your own thoughts as just noises. And soon you will find that the so-called outside world. And the so-called inside world come together. They are a happening. Your thoughts are a happening just like the sounds going on outside and everything is simply a happening, and all you are doing is watching it.
  • It's all just sound.
  • It's all just sound.
