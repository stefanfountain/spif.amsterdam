№ 04 / 14 Plate III · Vox Futuri
№ 02 — Work in development

Vox Futuri

In development · 2026

An oracle for one. Tune a brass dial; thirty years on, you appear on the screen and answer back.

Vintage brass-trimmed cockpit television. Stripped Soviet tank radio for the dial. Two-way mirror over an LCD; a hidden camera reads the face. A specialised edge-AI computer keeps the ritual running. The oracle does not predict — it gives you back to yourself, late.

Form
Single-participant installation
Materials
Brass cockpit TV · Soviet tank radio · 2-way mirror · Raspberry Pi 5 + Hailo-8 NPU
Lineage
Successor to Tardis (TNW, 2017)

Vox Ignis

Premiering · 30 May 2026

A 30-minute spoken-word and live electronic ritual, scored to a Tesla coil.

Guided from silence to movement — a search for the signal that lives beneath the everyday noise, and a glimpse of the one thing that counts. The Tesla coil is not a spectacle item; it is the night's permission slip to let the body remember what the body knows.

Meditation Station

A 1950s bakelite television, gutted and rewired with a quiet new heart.

Two rotary encoders sit where the tuning and volume used to live; a Raspberry Pi runs a custom interface behind the glass. Pull the cord, find your resonance.

№ 06 — Tardis · 20-minute ritual · 2017

The host.

A 20-minute participatory installation. Built by a five-person team and performed with hired actors.

The participant entered alone. They left holding a sentence — clear, concrete, theirs — that they didn't realise they already knew. The principle that Vox Futuri now builds the museum-grade apparatus for.
