№ 01 — On the work

An artist of
resonant systems.

Spif builds instruments to slow people down and lead them somewhere they did not expect to go — across modular performance, spoken-word ritual, AI installation, and tactile interactive objects.

The vocabulary is vintage hardware and electric ritual: bakelite cabinetry, brass-trimmed cockpit television, eurorack patch cables, two-way mirror, warm signal lamps. The intention is closer to ceremony than to spectacle — a museum bookshop's hush, with a Tesla coil quietly waiting in the wings.

Based in Amsterdam, working toward a permanent multidisciplinary space — Diepgang (Dutch for depth) — that holds the performances, the installations, and the workshops in one room.

Vox Arboris

ongoing
Live · A'dam TowerAmsterdam — April 2026 Live · OdonienCologne, DE Installation · WIHH GalleryAmsterdam — Summer 2023, one-month residency Live · Mayor ManorTen Club, Amsterdam — November 2022, one-month residency FormModular performance + gallery installation

Vox Arboris began as a month-long residency at Ten Club's Mayor Manor in November 2022, returned as a one-month installation at WIHH Gallery in 2023 — a slow, branching modular score the room learned by heart — and has since travelled: a live set at Odonien in Cologne, and high above the IJ at the A'dam Tower in April 2026. The piece treats the modular rig like a tree: signals enter the roots, take their time, and only certain frequencies make it to the canopy.

Vox Futuri In development · 2026

2026
in development

An oracle for one.
Tune a brass dial; a rendering of you, thirty years on, appears on the screen and answers.
The trick — proven by the analog predecessor — is that the future self knows nothing you don't.
The device just asks the questions that release the answer.

Vintage brass-trimmed cockpit television. Stripped Soviet tank radio for the dial. Two-way mirror over an LCD; a hidden camera reads the face. A Raspberry Pi underneath keeps the ritual running. The oracle does not predict — it gives you back to yourself, late.

Vox Ignis Premiering · 30 May 2026

2026
premiere

A 30-minute spoken-word and live electronic ritual. Guided from silence to movement — a search for the signal that lives beneath the everyday noise, and a glimpse, somewhere, of the one thing that counts.

An evening that holds the audience the way a good ceremony does: gradual descent, a long middle, a release that earns itself. Composed for modular synthesis, voice, and one act of light that arrives only when the room is ready.

Tardis

2017
VenueThe Next Web Conference · Amsterdam FormParticipatory psychological installation · 20 min TeamFive collaborators · hired actors

A 20-minute participatory installation. The participant travels five years into the future and meets the version of themselves that has already accomplished what they came to do. Built by a five-person team and performed with hired actors. The conceptual prototype for Vox Futuri.

№ 07

Cubic Reset — Unscramble Your Life

2015
VenueBrave Festival (~5000 attendees) · Tuinen van West · Amsterdam Scale3 × 3 metres StateLit from within at night

A three-metre interactive Rubik's cube installed in the gardens of Tuinen van West, Amsterdam — one corner deliberately skewed. Visitors stepped inside, chose a single topic to change in their life (finances, a relationship, a worry), and were led through a breathing exercise to release it. Press the RESET button to open the WINDOW to the rest of your life.
