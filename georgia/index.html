Georgia — Juta, two nights on Kazbek, and the green west

The decision is made. Mountains first on fresh legs — Juta, two Stepantsminda days (Chaukhi, then full Truso), then two nights at Altihut, 3,014 m on Kazbek's flank, with a whole unloaded glacier day between them. Down, west, and the trip decompresses: an air-conditioned Kutaisi base for the spas, caves, canyons and swims. Option B below keeps the same bones but trades the hut for a third Stepantsminda night and a Martvili village base — kept for comparison.

The shape in one line: 1 Tbilisi → 1 Juta → 2 Stepantsminda → 2 Altihut → 2 Kutaisi → 1 Tbilisi. The car exists only while it's driven (Day 3 morning → Day 10 evening), no drive day starts before ~09:30 (mountain mornings are the only alarms — the peaks cloud over by ~10:00), and the one monster day sits mid-trip where a problem costs a nap — not the 06:00 flight home.

I.The plan — Option A two hut nights + the Kutaisi base

Two consecutive Stepantsminda days come first — Chaukhi on arrival day, full Truso as the recovery day — then the hut, so legs are banked before the 1,300 m climb. No tent at any point: Altihut is a proper staffed hut — but know the real numbers (verified on their booking engine 2 Jul): ₾290 ≈ €95 pp per night half board in the 16-bed dorm, one private 4-person room (~€380/night whole-room) that books out first, duvets and linens provided, restaurant and bar — and no showers, power off 23:00–05:00. Book at altihut.ge/en/book or info@altitude.ge. Big bags wait at the Stepantsminda hotel and get collected on the Day-8 pass-through.

Option A — Juta and two Stepantsminda days, the climb to Altihut, then the western spokes from Kutaisi. One combined long day (Day 8), ~1,100 km driven.

Mountain fine print — verified 2 Jul 2026

  • Altitude: the Day-6 sleep jump (1,740 → 3,014 m) sits in the medium-AMS band — expect a real chance (~1 in 4) of a headachy, broken first hut night; the second is always better. Worth asking the huisarts about acetazolamide (Diamox) as cheap insurance, and go easy on the hut bar the first night. The safety valve: Stepantsminda is only 3 h downhill.
  • Early starts are non-negotiable on the three big hike days (Chaukhi, the hut climb, the glacier viewpoint): Kazbek and Chaukhi cloud over from ~10:00 and afternoon thunderstorms are the July pattern. ~07:30 out; afternoons are for arrival beers.
  • UV at 3,000 m+ is effectively extreme — SPF50 reapplied, category-3+ sunglasses, brimmed hats, SPF lip balm. Wind chill hides the burn.
  • Knees: ~3,000 m of cumulative descent across Days 4–8. Trekking poles are mandatory kit, and the Gergeti 4×4 taxi can absorb 430 m of the Day-8 descent.
  • Chaukhi snowfields: mid-July normally passable with poles, but ask at Fifth Season the evening before — early-July crossings report leftover snow near the pass.
  • Insurance: check your travel policy covers hiking to 3,500 m — Georgian mountain rescue is thin and evacuation from the hut zone can take many hours.

The cost: no hut nights, and the monster drive (Day 7) runs 30 min longer to reach Martvili.

III.Renting the car, without surprises

Five things to get right at booking

  • Book through Localrent (local agencies) or a vetted local firm — better prices (~€40–55/day incl. proper insurance), relaxed about mountain roads, and flexible on hotel delivery and collection at both ends.
  • Car exists only while you drive it — Day 3 morning to Day 10 evening (8 days): delivered to the Tbilisi hotel ~09:00 on Day 3 (€10–20), returned on arrival back in Tbilisi on Day 10 — office drop or hotel collection. No 5 am pickup on landing, no 03:45 airport drop, no city parking ever; Bolts cover both airport runs for ~30 GEL each.
  • A compact SUV is the sweet spot — the Juta gravel and village streets want clearance; real 4×4 is not required anywhere on this route. Truso's rough track stays a local-jeep job. But read the prohibited-routes clause: Localrent's standard Georgia terms ban driving beyond Sno toward Juta and beyond Almasiani into Truso (cars are GPS-tracked; insurance void off-list). Truso is already by local jeep here — for Juta, get written OK for the Sno→Juta road with your specific car, or have Fifth Season collect you in Sno (lodges arrange this routinely).
  • Insurance: full-coverage tier; windscreen/underbody claims are the classic Georgia gotchas. Photograph the car thoroughly at pickup. Dutch licence is fine; an IDP is cheap peace of mind.
  • The Military Highway is the Russia–Armenia freight artery: truck convoys at the Larsi border occasionally clog it. Cars usually thread through, but pad Days 3 and 7/8 and check georoad.ge each mountain morning.

V.Booking list, in firing order

  1. Lock the date window against the forecast first
    Dates are flexible — that's the plan's superpower. Book refundable tiers now, watch the 10-day Kazbegi forecast, and commit when Days 4–8 look clear: Chaukhi (Day 4) and the hut days (6–8) are the trip's heart.
  2. Altihut ×2 (Days 6–7), half board — and know it's non-refundable
    Verified live (2 Jul): dorm beds still free across mid-July windows, the one private room nearly gone. Cancellation is 100% inside ~21 days of arrival — so this booking, not the hotels, is what locks your dates. Decide the window first, then book hut + flights in the same hour at altihut.ge/en/book (info@altitude.ge). Dorm = 16 beds incl. pre-dawn summit parties; the private quad ~€380/night is the couple option if it's still there.
  3. Car: 8 days, hotel delivery ~09:00 Day 3 → return in Tbilisi on Day 10 evening
    Via Localrent. Confirm in writing: full coverage, hotel delivery, and evening return (office or hotel collection). July is peak.
  4. Fifth Season, Juta ×1 (Day 3)
    Re-date the doc's old 11→12 July hold — it's wrong for every version of this trip; cancel it while it's free.
  5. Stepantsminda ×2 (Days 4–6)
    Re-date your booking.com pick, and confirm the hotel stores your bags between checkout (Day 6 morning) and the Day-8 pass-through. (Option B: ×3, Days 4–7.)
  6. Kutaisi ×2 (Days 8–10): hotel with pool + AC
    The heat insurance — lowland July runs 30–34°C. (Option B instead: Martvili ×3, the doc's link, verify real AC.)
  7. Tbilisi bookends: arrival night with early check-in (no car — Bolt in) · final night anywhere central (no car either — it went back on Day 10)
    The first must let you in at 5 am; the second just needs a working doorbell at 03:15 for the Bolt to the airport.

VI.Library — everything scouted, for this trip or the next

The research from all the earlier drafts, kept as a menu. Nothing here is in the plan; all of it is proven doable from earlier revisions of this document.

Svaneti — the road not taken

  • Mestia — tower-village hub; ~6½–7 h drive or the Vanilla Sky hop from Tbilisi
  • Mestia → Ushguli trek — the 3-day cut: Zhabeshi · Adishi · over the 2,720 m pass, jeep home (guide)
  • Ushguli — UNESCO towers under Shkhara, Europe's highest year-round village
  • Koruldi Lakes · Chalaadi glacier — Mestia's two big day hikes
  • Vanilla Sky — 30-min Tbilisi↔Mestia flights, 15 kg cap, no Saturdays (tickets)
  • Tbilisi–Zugdidi day train — 08:36→12:55, Mon–Fri, ~13 GEL (GR); sleeper suspended until ~2027

Kazbegi & Khevsureti extras

  • Betlemi hut (3,653 m) — the step above Altihut; Day 7's turn-around point if legs sing
  • Juta → Roshka through-hike — over the Chaukhi pass to the Abudelauri coloured lakes; needs a driver shuttle, not your own car
  • Khada valley — tower-dotted side valley off the Military Highway, quick stop
  • Gudauri + Friendship Monument — viewpoint on the highway, free
  • Shatili–Mutso (Khevsureti) — medieval fortress towns; rough road, rentals mostly banned — hired 4×4 trip

East — wine country

  • Sighnaghi — walled hilltop wine town over the Alazani valley, 1½–2 h from Tbilisi
  • Bodbe monastery — St Nino's resting place, 10 min from Sighnaghi
  • Qvevri wineries — the Georgia headline this route only tastes in a Tbilisi wine bar; Kakheti deserves 2 nights another time
  • Tusheti (Omalo–Dartlo–Shenako) — researched in depth 2 Jul 2026 after a local's tip: cool 20–26°C days, slate-tower villages, "what Svaneti was 20 years ago". Pass opened late this year (20 Jun); park the rental in Kvemo Alvani, shared jeep up 100–120 GEL/seat (departs ~11:00–15:00 — no early start), guesthouses ~80–120 GEL pp half board, Magti-only signal, cash only. Verdict: needs 3 nights + 1–2 buffer days (July closures have precedent: 3–5 Jul 2025) — doesn't fit 9 nights without gutting the hut + west. The anchor of the next Georgia trip, paired with Kakheti wine below.
  • The next-trip blueprint: Tusheti + Kakheti (~8–9 nights) — Tbilisi 1 · Telavi/Alvani 1 (Alaverdi monastery) · Omalo/Dartlo 3 · descend · Kakheti wine 2–3 (Twins Wine Cellar Napareuli, Pheasant's Tears Sighnaghi, pool at Lopota or Schuchmann) · Tbilisi 1 — the wine block doubles as the pass-closure buffer
  • Lagodekhi — Black Rock Lake — genuine 3-day wilderness loop, ranger shelters

West — spares near the base

  • Okatse canyon + Kinchkha waterfall — the backup canyon day, ~1 h from Kutaisi
  • Gelati monastery — UNESCO, 20 min from Kutaisi (Option A's Day-10 morning option)
  • Bagrati cathedral — Kutaisi's hilltop landmark, evening stroll
  • Kaghu waterfall + Balda canyon — the Martvili swims; a full lazy day in Option B, a Day-10 morning option in A
  • Legends Tskaltubo Spa Resort — the renovated sanatorium hotel, if a spa-town sleep ever tempts
  • Bathhouse No. 6 — the working radon baths, daily 10:00–17:00, ~25 GEL/cabin
